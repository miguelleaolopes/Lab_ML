Classification Task 1
We were given 50x50 images of butterfly wings with two types of wing patterns: spots and
eye-spots for this homework. The purpose was to categorize these two types of patterns as 0
(spots) or 1 (eye-spots).
There are several strategies for this kind of classification tasks, in our case we tried several
CNN’s with different architectures, random forest and svm.

Final Report Machine Learning
This architecture was created using a mix of research and adaption of popular models such
as Alex-Net, VGG, and LeNet. We arrived at the table arrangement by repeating a normal
configuration of 2D Convolution, 2D MaxPolling, Dropout, and eventually Batch Normalization,
followed by a Flatten in between some of these cycles. Adding extra layers at this time was
significantly increasing our computation cost with negligible gain. Because we were dealing with
binary classification, the final activation layer was a sigmoid, which produced better results than
softmax and others.
The design was then constructed using an Adam optimizer with a 1E − 3 learning rate and
a loss parameter called ”binary crossentropy.” These values were determined by trial and error
utilizing all of the Keras options. In order to speed up learning and converge quicker, the data
comprised of the RGB channel values of each pixel in the picture. On top of that, we added data
augmentation such as rotation and modest degrees of zoom to certain training data.
3.2. Results analysis and conclusions
After submitting the results, the professors assigned an F1 Score of 0.919925512, indicating
that there was plenty of room for growth. For one thing, the model’s architecture could probably
be improved further by changing some of the layers; additionally, this model is very susceptible to
statistical fluctuations that can influence the final results; a better approach would be to take an
average of multiple runs of the same model to improve the confidence of more difficult images to
classify.
